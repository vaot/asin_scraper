Product description

Style:Jeep Wrangler JK (2007-2018)  |  Color:Black Twill

Bestop's new and unique Sunrider for Hardtop let's you replace those bulky Wrangler JK Freedom Panels with versatile year-round flexibility. Folding design lets you flip the panel back for an open-sky ride, or simply pull it forward, latch it up for protection from the elements. Includes assembled folding frame with fabric, and custom door rails for quick, bolt-on installation. Removes quickly if desired. Available in our gorgeous black acrylic fabric or OEM quality black diamond sailcloth fabric. For easier installation, the top should be installed at a temperature above 72° F. Below this temperature, the fabric may contract an inch or more, making it difficult to fit the vehicle.

5.0 out of 5 starsYou need one, buy it NOW!
May 10, 2016
Style: Jeep Wrangler JK (2007-2018)Color: Black Diamond
Like everyone else, I was getting really tired of dealing with the bulky Freedom panels when removed. You don't dare go anywhere without them, because if it rains or suddenly gets super cold you're screwed. In their storage bag, it creates a package 32 x 38 x 7 inches. I have trail gear in the back in boxes, and there's just no space for the things, besides which if a rain cloud threatens, it's 20 minutes on the side of the road putting them reinstalled. You end up just leaving the things on even in nice weather because of the hassle. Compare that to 10 seconds of flipping the Sunrider roof shut and popping two latches closed. It's a no brainer. The Sunrider top also works with the Spiderweb sunshade. I leave the corners of the shade loose, they don't flap, and you can’t see them because the sun visors hold them up. The ball straps don't get in the way of the Sunrider's windshield clamps because they pull to one side of the latch-loop. It changes how you open & close the top, you have to get out and reach in thru the corners of the Spiderweb shade to open or shut the top, but that adds all of 15 extra seconds, and the mesh shade is very nice to have as it blocks the heat of direct sun and cuts down wind blast. Don't need it? Roll it up out of the way for totally open driving. Quality of the Sunrider top is typical Bestop; no corner cutting here, it’s top quality all the way. Material is heavy cloth with vinyl finish. (Maybe Denier 800?) No flapping, no wind noise, no drafts. It sticks up a bit when open, but doesn’t add wind noise or flapping at all. And, when closed, it "balloons" slightly as you close the doors, ending that irritating "never fully latched" door syndrome in every JKU. The air relief valve (behind the tire carrier) isn't large enough to let enough air out as the last door closes, so that last door just about always goes first latch only, making you reopen and SLAM the door shut. (Or roll a window down an inch) It's especially annoying with passengers who never seem to get the door shut. The flex of the Sunrider top helps this problem A LOT. I installed mine by myself, but a second person would make it go easier. Getting bolts to line up was a bit hard due to going thru the roll bar padding. NOTE: Get on a ladder and check the seal around the back of the deck piece. (Where it mates with the hard top) Make sure it tucks in everywhere before you tighten the mushroom bolts, you don’t want it pooching out anywhere. The deck part should fit almost flush with the hard top surface, and it won’t if the seal goes past that mating line. Carefully “roll in” the two door parts so the seals mate up nicely at each end. If you have the Spiderweb shade, make sure the grommets in the two straps are lined up so the mushroom bolts go thru them.
